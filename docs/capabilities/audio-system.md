# <i data-lucide="volume-2"></i> Audio System Architecture

> **TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS** | **SYSTEM: AUDIO HUB** | **MODEL: DFPLAYER MINI / TPA3118**


This guide explains the audio stack for the Wee2-D2 project. It covers the sound hub, animation audio triggers, and the high-fidelity amplification system managed by Node 2.


---


## Hardware Components (Audio Hub)

The audio system is centralized in the body of the droid. It uses a specialized MP3 player and a Class-D amplifier to deliver high-volume cinematic sound through a single 3.5" driver.


- **Sound Module**: DFPlayer Mini (MP3-TF-16P)
- **Amplifier**: TPA3118 60W Mono (PBTL Mode)
- **Speaker**: Pyle 3.5" Full-Range (4 Ohm)
- **Power**: 5V Logic Rail (Mini560 Pro)


---


## Animation Audio Triggers (ESP-NOW Mesh)

Audio triggers are generated by **Node 1 (Dome Master)** and relayed wirelessly to **Node 2 (Sound Hub)** using the ESP-NOW mesh protocol. This prevents motor nose from interfering with the analog audio signals.


These triggers are verified in the `v2.6.0-Dashboard` firmware sequence.


| Sound CMD | Function | Result | Citation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0x01** | **Idle Chirp** | Random personality beep | [node-1.yaml:81](../../firmware/production/node-1-dome-motion.yaml#L81) |
| **0x02** | **Angry Tantrum** | High-pitched rapid scream | [node-1.yaml:81](../../firmware/production/node-1-dome-motion.yaml#L81) |
| **0x03** | **Dance Groove** | Synchronized dance music | [node-1.yaml:81](../../firmware/production/node-1-dome-motion.yaml#L81) |
| **0x04** | **Cantina Band** | Full cinematic theme | [node-1.yaml:81](../../firmware/production/node-1-dome-motion.yaml#L81) |
| **0x99** | **Stop Sound** | Immediate silence (E-Stop) | [node-1.yaml:81](../../firmware/production/node-1-dome-motion.yaml#L81) |


---


1. **Trigger Reception**: Node 2 receives a 1-byte command (e.g., `0x01`) over the 2.4GHz ESP-NOW mesh.
2. **Serial Dispatch**: Node 2 sends the UART trigger to the DFPlayer Mini at 9600 baud.
3. **Amplification**: The DFPlayer sends a line-level analog signal to the TPA3118, which is then amplified to the 60W speaker system.


---


## Practical Maintenance

Ensure the MicroSD card is formatted as FAT32 and that all files are named sequentially (001.mp3, 002.mp3, etc.) within their folders.


- **Audio Popping**: If you hear "popping" during motor movement, check the star-grounding near the central negative bus bar.
- **Volume Limit**: The TPA3118 is powerful enough to damage the 3.5" driver if the gain is set too high. Use the Node 2 dashboard to cap the volume at 80%.
- **Heat**: The Mono amplifier can get warm during long playbacks of the Cantina Band. Ensure adequate airflow in the body cavity.
